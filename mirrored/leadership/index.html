The Green Team Task (team) and Green Check Leadership Focus (individual) summaries also included below.

Key Terms & Definitions:

  • Green Team, Gray Team & Red Team – 3 teams that exist in every team culture. Goal is to Bulid Green, Sway the Gray, and Remove the Red.
  • Lifeguard – athletes that help prevent teammates from “drowning” into the Red Team by looking for Red Team Warning Signs
  • Red Team Warning Signs – Indicators that point to an athlete getting closer to the Red Team
  • Assess vs Act – After identifying a Red Team Warning Sign, Lifeguards must determine whether to sit back and assess the situation, or “blow the whistle” and act – helping bring that athlete back to Green as best as they can

TOPIC: Active Listening & Including Everyone

Here is a link to view the meeting presentation PDF

May Action Items:

  • Individual Leader Focus:
      • Practice active listening at swim practice. If you coaches are talking, be sure you are attentive and remember what they said.
      • Challenge for the month – don’t ask any questions about the set, remember it the first time it’s said.
      • Link to YouTube video we watched on Active Listening 
  • Team Action Item
      • After each dryland session, do a quick cheer/breakdown with your team. You can do something as simple as “FAMILY ON 3, 1, 2, 3..”.
      • Get a new teammate to lead this cheer every day – could be the person with best effort, loudest encouragement, or someone that normally doesn’t get the spotlight.

TOPIC: Leading by Example

Meeting Recap & Presentation: Here is a link to view the meeting presentation PDF

Here is a link to WATCH a short recap of our April meeting

April Action Items:

  • Individual Leader Focus: Take every opportunity you can to lead by example and do the things we listed not he previous slide.
  • Team Meeting during Dryland
    • In this team meeting, you will partner up with someone you trust. This will be your accountability partner.
    • Share with your partner how you can START, STOP, and KEEP being a better example. If you would like, you can ask your partner to tell you what they think you should START, STOP, and KEEP to be a better example.
    • From here, you should hold your accountability partner to what they said they want to START, STOP, and KEEP doing.
    • Remember that feedback is only to make each other better

TOPIC: Building Mansions of Success

Meeting Recap & Presentation: Here is a link to view the meeting presentation PDF

Here is a link to WATCH a short recap of what we discussed this month

February Action item:

  • Individual Action Item: Lead the charge with the habits that you and your team come up with below!
  • Team Action Item: Team Meeting during Dryland
    • Split into groups of 3-5 and come up with 2 habits that you need to have in swimming to be a better athlete & teammate. Then come up with 2 habits that are common among the team that need to stop.
    • After a few minutes, come back together as a team & have 1 person from each group share what they wrote down.
    • As a team, vote on the top 2 great habits, and top 2 bad habits that we want to stop.
    • Have teammates either verbally agree to implement these habit changes, or even have everyone sign a paper and sign off on it.

TOPIC: Servant Leadership / Investment season

Meeting Recap & Presentation: Here is a link to view the meeting presentation PDF

Here is a link to WATCH one of the November Meetings on Youtube

If you missed the meeting, review the presentation & write down answers to all the questions in the presentation.

November Action Item:

  • Individual Action Item: Work on selfless (servant) leadership skills. Try to do 1 selfless act each day at practice for your teammates candor coaches. We listed a few things we could do to be more selfless to our team in the meetings, the most common were:
    • Show respect & empathy to teammates
    • Give encouragement, even if you’re tired
    • Actively listen & understand what coaches are saying
    • Help teammates with equipment or figuring out the set
    • Bring a good attitude to practice
  • Team Action Item: Team Meeting during Dryland
    • You will be given 1 station in dryland  to meet with your team. You may take 2 stations or have a separate meeting time if you need more time.
    • In this meeting your team will come up with 3 group goals for the season. If you already have group goals, you can skip this step
    • After coming up with your goals, talk about the following 3 questions with your team:
      • What should we START doing to help reach our goals?
      • What should we STOP doing to help reach our goals?
      • What should we KEEP doing to help reach our goals?

Meeting Recap & Presentation: Here is a link to view the meeting presentation PDF

October Action Item:

  • Individual Leader Focus: Be a positive Thermostat & say 1 R.I.V.E.R. statement each day to an athlete & coach.
  • Team Action Item: Team Meeting during Dryland
    • Meet with your team during dryland between Oct. 16th-Oct 21st
    • This meeting will be led by the members of the leadership group. In this meeting, have everyone in the practice group say 1 quality of a good teammate.

TOPIC: Don’t React, Respond / Building The Capacity to Endure

Meeting Recap & Presentation: Here is a link to view the meeting presentation PDF

If you missed the meeting, review the presentation & write down answers to all the questions in bold. We discussed the following chapters in our meeting from “The Champion Teammate”:

  • Chapter 11: Don’t React, Respond
  • Chapter 12: Build the Capacity to Endure More

January Action Item:

  • Individual Leader Focus: Keep a journal or Notes log of situations where you react. Later that night before going to bed write down how you could have responded instead in that situation.  Vice versa, if you respond, write down how you were able to control your emotions & not react.
  • Team Action Item (IF IN THE CLUB TEAM PROGRAM): Meet with your team anytime after January 22nd
    • Talk to your team about how competence builds confidence. Recite the 3 tips for building competence to your team, and talk about what each means:
        • “It’s not about positive thinking, it’s about taking positive action no matter what you fear or feel”
        • “It’s not about building pressure, it’s about building the capacity to embrace more”
        • “It’s not about motivation, it’s about connecting to what matters.”
    • With your team, create something that you all will say to each other when practice gets tough and the time to build competence comes
      • Whether it be a mantra/phrase you say to each other, or maybe a pact of always racing & giving 100% effort during the hardest moments of a main set
  • Read Chapters 13-17 in “The Champion Teammate” to prepare for February.

TOPIC: Be an Elevator / Be a Thermostat, not a Thermometer

Meeting Recap & Presentation: Here is a link to view the meeting presentation PDF

If you missed the meeting, review the presentation & write down answers to all the questions in bold. We discussed the following chapters in our meeting from “The Champion Teammate”:

  • Chapter 1: Be an Elevator
  • Chapter 4: Be a Thermostat, not a Thermometer

October Action Item:

  • Individual Leader Focus: Try to set a goal to say 1 R.I.V.E.R. statement each day to a coach or teammate, & check out page 40 in the Champion Teammate for a checklist of how to be a better influence on your team!
